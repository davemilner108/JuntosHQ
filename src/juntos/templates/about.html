About JuntosHQ

Franklin's Original Junto (1727)

In 1727, a young Benjamin Franklin gathered twelve friends in Philadelphia to form the Junto — a club devoted to mutual improvement. Every Friday evening, members met to discuss questions of morals, politics, and natural philosophy. Each member was expected to produce essays, propose community projects, and hold one another accountable for personal growth.

The group's only requirements were a sincere love of truth, a desire to help others, and a willingness to put skin in the game. Franklin credited the Junto with sparking many of his greatest civic achievements: the first lending library, volunteer fire company, public hospital, and the University of Pennsylvania.

Twelve Members, One Purpose

Franklin deliberately kept each Junto small — no more than twelve members. A group that size is large enough for diverse perspectives yet intimate enough that every voice is heard and every member is held to account. When demand grew, Franklin didn't expand the original club; he encouraged members to start new juntos of their own, creating a network of small groups working toward the common good.

Carrying the Spirit Forward

JuntosHQ brings Franklin's model into the present day. We help communities organize into groups of twelve committed members — people with skin in the game who meet regularly, ask hard questions, and take action together. Whether improving a neighborhood, launching a civic project, or simply becoming better citizens, the junto structure keeps groups focused, accountable, and effective.

The world doesn't need bigger committees. It needs more juntos.
